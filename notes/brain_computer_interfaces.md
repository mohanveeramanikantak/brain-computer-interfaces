# Brain-Computer Interfaces – Detailed Notes

## 1. Introduction
Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI) are systems that allow communication between the brain and external devices without using traditional physical pathways like muscles.

---

## 2. Working Principle
BCI systems follow these steps:
1. Signal acquisition (EEG, implants)
2. Signal preprocessing (noise removal)
3. Feature extraction
4. Classification using ML
5. Output generation

---

## 3. Types of BCI

### 3.1 Invasive BCI
- Implanted directly into brain
- High accuracy
- Risky surgery required

### 3.2 Non-Invasive BCI
- Uses EEG headsets
- Safe and easy to use
- Lower accuracy

### 3.3 Semi-Invasive BCI
- Implanted outside brain tissue
- Moderate accuracy

---

## 4. Technologies Used
- EEG (Electroencephalography)
- Machine Learning
- Signal Processing
- Neural Networks

---

## 5. Applications
- Control prosthetic limbs
- Help paralyzed patients communicate
- Brain-controlled wheelchairs
- Gaming and VR

---

## 6. Advantages
- Improves quality of life
- Enables new human-computer interaction
- Assists disabled individuals

---

## 7. Challenges
- Signal noise
- High cost
- Ethical concerns
- Data privacy issues

---

## 8. Future Scope
- Brain-controlled smart homes
- Memory enhancement
- Integration with AI systems

---

## 9. Career Scope
- BCI Developer
- Neuroscientist
- Biomedical Engineer

---

## 10. Conclusion
BCI is a rapidly growing field that has the potential to transform healthcare and human interaction with technology.
